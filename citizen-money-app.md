## Business Case

Monarch Money is great at showing you your money. They categorize, visualize, track. But they stop short of telling you what to do about it. The gap is:

- You see you spent $800 on dining — app says nothing
- You're off-budget in 3 categories — app shows red, offers no guidance
- You have $2,000 sitting in checking — no prompting to move it
- Your subscription total crept up $40/month — no alert, no action suggestion
  An AI layer on top of personal finance data turns passive reporting into active coaching — the difference between a dashboard and an advisor.
  Who this is for: The mass-market consumer who can't afford a financial advisor but wants more than a spreadsheet. 25-45, dual income, some savings, vague financial anxiety.

## Core Features

1. Transaction sync + categorization

- Plaid pulls transactions from connected accounts
- AI re-categorizes ambiguous transactions (Plaid's categories are mediocre)
- User can correct; corrections train future categorization

2. AI Chat — "Ask your money anything"

- RAG over the user's own financial data
- Natural language queries:
    - "How much did I spend on groceries last 3 months?"
    - "Am I on track to hit my vacation savings goal?"
    - "What's my biggest spending change vs last month?"
- Agent has tools: query transactions, calculate trends, compare periods

3. Proactive insight engine

- Runs on a schedule (daily/weekly)
- Detects and surfaces:
    - Unusual charges (potential fraud or billing errors)
    - Subscription creep (new recurring charges)
    - Overspend vs budget by category
    - Savings opportunities ("you have $3k sitting in checking earning nothing")
    - Bill timing issues ("3 large bills hit same day, watch cash flow")

4. Weekly financial digest

- Plain-English narrative summary generated by AI
- "This week you spent 20% more than your average, mostly on home improvement. You're ahead on your emergency fund goal. One new subscription appeared — looks like it might be a free trial."
- Delivered via email or in-app

5. Goal tracking with AI check-ins

- User sets goals (emergency fund, vacation, pay off card)
- AI tracks progress and adjusts projections dynamically
- Proactively surfaces: "At your current savings rate you'll hit your goal in 8 months, not 6 — here's why"

6. Subscription audit agent

- Scans for recurring charges
- Groups and surfaces total monthly subscription spend
- Flags duplicates or unused-looking services
- One-click "remind me to cancel this"

## The AI Architecture

User Query
↓
Conversational agent (LangGraph)
↓
Tool router decides which tools to call:
├── query_transactions(filters, date_range)
├── calculate_budget_variance(category, period)
├── get_account_balances()
├── get_goals_progress()
├── detect_recurring_charges()
└── compare_periods(current, previous)
↓
Agent synthesizes tool results
↓
Plain-English response with numbers grounded in real data
The insight engine is a separate scheduled agent:
Cron trigger (daily)
↓
Insight orchestrator runs sub-agents:
├── Anomaly detector
├── Subscription scanner
├── Budget variance checker
├── Savings opportunity finder
↓
Deduplicates against already-surfaced insights
↓
Stores new insights → pushes notifications

## One Honest Consideration

The Plaid connection and financial data handling raises the bar on security and trust — users are linking bank accounts. That's not a dealbreaker for a portfolio project, but worth being intentional about:

- Don't store raw credentials
- Be clear in the UI it's a demo/personal tool
- Plaid's sandbox mode lets you build the whole thing without real accounts
